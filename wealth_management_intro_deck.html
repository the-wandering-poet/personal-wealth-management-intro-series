Pick your own

Single stock

Buy individual company shares directly. Full control over selection and timing; no fund layer between you and the company.

Key trade-off

No fund fees, but you bear concentration risk, and your odds of beating the index are at best the same as professional active managers.

Track the index

Passive

Vehicles built to replicate an index, including ETFs and index mutual funds. Transparent rules, broad diversification, low cost.

Key trade-off

Accepts market return with no alpha attempt. In efficient markets this is the bar most active managers fail to clear.

Hire a manager

Active

Active ETFs and active mutual funds use manager discretion or systematic signals aimed at delivering excess return.

Key trade-off

Pays for the chance of outperformance, but data shows the majority of active funds underperform after fees, and it gets worse over time.

1. Capture employer match

Action: contribute to your 401(k) at least up to the full company match.

It is part of your salary package; skipping it is leaving compensation unused.

2. Max Roth IRA

2026 quota: $7,500 under age 50, subject to income eligibility.

Use it for broad equity ETFs or low-cost index funds; qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

3. Add taxable brokerage

Action: automate monthly buys after emergency cash and retirement contributions.

Keep it tax-efficient: broad ETFs, low turnover, and avoid frequent realized gains.

4. Keep it boring

Default: target-date fund, total-market ETF, or S&P 500 ETF.

Avoid turning early salary into concentrated employer stock or speculative trades.

Rule of thumb for selecting hedge fund products

Hedge funds can diversify equity risk, but product selection matters more than the label.

  • Check historical returns, especially drawdowns; drawdowns should be lower than annualized returns.
  • Ask for after-fee returns, since management and performance fees can materially reduce investor outcomes.
  • Diversify across multiple funds to reduce manager and strategy risk; this can be achieved through a fund of funds.

Closed-end funds and listed private-market wrappers can trade far above or below NAV. Paying a premium means future returns must overcome fund performance and possible premium compression; liquidity can vanish when sentiment reverses while private holdings remain hard to value or sell.

Retail DCA vehicle for gold exposure

For regular investing, use listed physical gold ETFs or ETCs designed to track spot gold directly, ideally backed by allocated bullion and priced close to the local spot gold benchmark. Examples include GLD, IAU, GLDM, and SGOL.

  • Prefer spot-linked physical gold ETFs/ETCs over gold miners, broad commodity funds, futures funds, leveraged products, or structured notes.
  • Check tracking error, expense ratio, liquidity, bid-ask spread, tax treatment, and whether units are physically backed.
  • DCA can smooth entry timing, but it does not remove gold's volatility or currency exposure.

What bonds are for

Bonds are productive assets with fixed cash flows. They are most useful when the portfolio needs stability, liquidity, and known funding dates.

  • Match duration to when you need the money.
  • Diversify credit risk; avoid reaching for yield blindly.
  • Use low-cost funds or ladders for core exposure.

Simple implementation

0-1 yr
Short
1-3 yrs
Core
3-7 yrs

For near-term liabilities, favor Treasury bills, short-duration government bonds, or high-quality bond funds. For return-seeking credit exposure, size high-yield or emerging-market bonds as risk assets rather than cash substitutes.

The "Rule of 20"

1% in annual cost ≈ 20% of lifetime wealth.

Multiply any ongoing annual cost by ~20 to see its present-value drag over a typical investing lifetime.
